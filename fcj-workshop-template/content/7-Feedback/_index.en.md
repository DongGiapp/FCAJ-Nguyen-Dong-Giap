# Sharing and Feedback

After participating in the **AWS First Cloud AI Journey** program, I gained practical exposure to AWS, cloud architecture, serverless, and current AI trends. The program was valuable because it did not only provide technical knowledge, but also encouraged students to research, practice, write documentation, and connect with the community.

## Overall Evaluation

### 1. Program Content

The program content was relevant to my learning and career direction. Topics such as AWS Lambda, API Gateway, DynamoDB, Amazon Bedrock, CloudFront, MCP, and multi-agent systems are closely connected to current real-world technology needs.

By building the proposal, workshop, and blog sections, I had the opportunity to organize my knowledge more systematically. Instead of only reading separate materials, I needed to understand the content, select the key ideas, and present them in a way that others could follow.

### 2. Learning Environment and Community

I appreciate the open learning environment of the FCAJ community. The Community Day events allowed me to explore new topics, listen to experienced speakers, and better understand how AWS and AI are applied in real-world scenarios.

The community atmosphere is friendly and encourages asking questions, sharing knowledge, and learning from one another. This makes cloud topics, which can be broad and complex for beginners, easier to approach.

### 3. Skills Gained

During the report preparation process, I improved several skills:

- Reading and understanding English technical materials.
- Summarizing and writing AWS blog content.
- Presenting technical content using Markdown and Hugo.
- Organizing a workshop into step-by-step sections.
- Strengthening my understanding of serverless backend with API Gateway, Lambda, and DynamoDB.
- Expanding my perspective on AI agents, LLMs, prompt routing, and cloud operations.

### 4. What I Appreciated Most

What I appreciated most was that the program helped me produce a relatively complete report, including proposal, workshop, blogs, event recaps, self-assessment, and feedback. Through this process, I did not only learn AWS knowledge but also practiced documenting, organizing, and communicating what I learned.

I also enjoyed the FCAJ Community Day events because the topics were up to date, closely related to AI/cloud trends, and included many practical examples.

## Suggestions for Improvement

### 1. More Milestone-Based Guidance

For students who are new to AWS, a clearer checklist for each stage such as proposal, workshop, blog, event, and self-assessment would make the report process easier to follow.

For example:

- Week 1-2: choose a topic and write the proposal.
- Week 3-6: build the workshop.
- Week 7-9: write blogs and document events.
- Week 10-12: finalize the report, review content, and polish presentation.

### 2. Midterm Review Session

A midterm review session would help students receive early feedback on their direction, workshop structure, blog content, and presentation style. This would reduce the risk of discovering missing or misaligned content near the end of the program.

### 3. More Practical Examples

Topics such as IAM, API Gateway, Lambda, DynamoDB, Bedrock, or MCP would be easier to understand with more small demos, architecture diagrams, or short labs. Practical examples help students connect theory with real AWS implementation.

## Future Expectations

In the future, I hope the program will include more sessions about:

- Serverless architecture in production.
- Amazon Bedrock and AI agents.
- AWS security and cost optimization.
- DevOps, observability, and CloudWatch.
- Building a cloud portfolio for students.

If I have the opportunity, I would like to continue participating in FCAJ community activities to learn new knowledge, stay updated with technology trends, and connect with others who are interested in AWS and AI.

## Conclusion

Overall, the AWS First Cloud AI Journey program brought me value in both technical knowledge and self-learning skills. After participating, I feel more confident approaching AWS services, better understand serverless architecture, and have more motivation to continue pursuing cloud and AI in the future.
